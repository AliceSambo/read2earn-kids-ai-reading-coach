# Read2Earn Kids — approved product experience

## Positioning

Read2Earn Kids turns every book into a **Living Reading Mission** where children read, receive supportive help, demonstrate real understanding, and build a trusted record of what they learned.

The competition prototype is one exceptional learning journey—not the entire future platform. It uses one locally stored story and proves the signature loop:

1. A child creates a nickname and names a companion.
2. The child enters Story Forest from an interactive learning-world map.
3. They choose **Read by myself**, **Read with my companion**, or **Listen and follow**.
4. Difficult words reveal syllables, a child-friendly meaning, and pronunciation.
5. The child explains the story by voice or text.
6. Supportive feedback checks meaning rather than exact phrasing.
7. Demonstrated understanding changes the story world and earns a Knowledge Gem with evidence.
8. The child rates enjoyment with five expressive faces (stored as 2, 4, 6, 8, or 10).
9. A grown-up sees a meaningful learning report and next activity.

## Companion

The child selects one of four companion appearances and provides a custom name during first-time setup. The UI screens unsafe terms locally, limits length, and defaults to “Kiko” only when blank. The nickname and companion name remain on-device. A production rename flow requires adult approval.

## Experience principles

- A premium animated story universe, not a colourful school database.
- Motion communicates state and progress; it is optional through reduced-motion mode.
- Rewards prove learning rather than encourage empty engagement.
- Feedback is warm, specific, and never shaming.
- Natural stopping points protect well-being.
- The child interface is playful; the adult report is calm and evidence-first.

## Future platform, not prototype scope

The longer roadmap includes public/free and premium books, school-controlled private libraries, verified students, teacher assignments, offline learning, multi-subject content, family challenges, parent-approved achievement cards, institution competitions, subscriptions, author tools, and parent-controlled non-transferable digital collectibles. These are documented direction, not unfinished prototype features.
