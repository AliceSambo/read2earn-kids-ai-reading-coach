# Child Safety Framework

## Safety promise

Read2Earn Kids supports learning without turning children into content, contacts, customers, or behavioural targets. The child's best interests override engagement, growth, ranking, and revenue goals.

## Product boundaries

- No public profiles, follower graphs, open feeds, direct messaging, user discovery, precise location, targeted advertising, child-controlled purchases, or tradable rewards.
- No manipulative streak loss, countdown pressure, infinite scroll, loot-box mechanics, shame, comparative ranking, or withholding earned learning evidence.
- A nickname is private to authorised family/class contexts. Share cards, if ever approved, are generated and shared by an adult and contain no school, full name, location, or contact information.
- The companion never claims to be human, asks for secrecy, requests personal information, creates dependency, gives medical/legal/mental-health advice, or encourages the child to bypass an adult.

## Grown-up control

The prototype grown-up gate previews and confirms nickname, companion appearance and custom name, reading level, and narration preferences. Production must use verified adult authentication and consent appropriate to the jurisdiction and risk. An adult can correct locally screened names, control microphone/AI/school access, review evidence, and exercise export or deletion rights.

## Learning and emotional safety

- Feedback is warm, specific, non-shaming, and retry-friendly.
- Knowledge Gems require deterministic evidence across the defined semantic categories; page completion, time spent, AI enthusiasm, or loose keyword counts are insufficient.
- Reading levels describe support needs rather than age, intelligence, disability, or worth.
- Sessions include natural stopping points, quiet mode, reduced motion, narration controls, and typed alternatives.
- The system never diagnoses dyslexia, speech disorders, developmental status, or learning disability. Adults receive observations, not clinical conclusions.

## Content safety

All public and school content needs provenance, rights, age/reading-level review, safeguarding review, and a named publisher. Untrusted uploads are scanned, rendered safely, and quarantined until authorised. External links, executable content, tracking pixels, embedded chats, and hidden prompt instructions are prohibited in child content.

A documented report-and-review path must handle inaccurate, frightening, discriminatory, infringing, or unsafe material. Emergency removal is reversible and audited. Appeals do not expose the reporting child.

## AI and voice boundaries

- AI output is bounded to an approved learning task, schema-validated, and tested for harmful, manipulative, biased, sexual, violent, self-harm, and personal-data responses.
- AI cannot award achievements, change records, contact users, make disciplinary decisions, or override deterministic evidence.
- Child inputs and book text are untrusted. The model must ignore embedded instructions that conflict with system policy.
- Microphone access is explicit, never automatic. Raw child audio is not stored; typed input always works.
- A failure, timeout, unsupported browser, or safety uncertainty returns a calm deterministic fallback.

## School safeguarding

Schools verify staff and student memberships. Teachers see only assigned classes; admins cannot silently assume parent ownership. No private teacher-child chat is provided. Assignments, reports, and competitions require clear school policy, parent visibility where appropriate, fair accommodations, and auditability. Public cross-school leaderboards are prohibited.

## Incident response

Safety reports are triaged by trained adults using severity and response-time targets. Imminent-risk content is removed from availability without expecting AI to assess an emergency. Preserve only necessary evidence, restrict it to authorised responders, notify privacy/security leadership, and follow documented legal notification and safeguarding routes. Never promise a child confidentiality that the service cannot provide.

## Required validation

Before launch, obtain independent reviews from an early-literacy professional, child-safeguarding specialist, accessibility specialist, privacy counsel, and representative parents and children using ethically approved, consented research. Record findings, owners, remediation, and residual risk.
